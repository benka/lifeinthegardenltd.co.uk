Gardening

Spending your free time in a beautiful garden is a great way to relax and recharge, but keeping it clean and well shaped can be time-consuming. We are here to help should you need a complete overhaul or just an extra pair of hands to bring harmony to your garden.

Garden Clearance

Our garden clearance service is for areas that need to be cleared for landscaping or other construction, for neglected or overgrown gardens.

We break up and remove patios, dismantle sheds, take apart concrete paths, demolish garden walks, etc. We also clear any kind of weeds, bushes, plants, trees, old turf or trunks and roots. We can clear areas quickly and efficiently, disposing of the green waste into licenced recycling centres.

The work is quicker if there is direct access to the garden. Otherwise the waste from the garden will need to be carried through the property. In these cases we cover your floor and protect walks and furniture. We will of course clean the area before the job is finished and keep disruption to a minimum.

Garden clearance waste can be bagged up and left on the site for council collection. Or we can take it straight for an additional charge. For waste disposal informations please see our price list below. With large volumes it could be necessary to hire a skip or a lorry. We arrange waste disposal, skip hire and road permit for you if required.

For smaller garden clearance projects we are able to discuss and quote the job on phone. We suggest to arrange a site meeting if the job takes longer than one or two days or need more worker than one or two.

Driveways: are one of the most popular landscaping work people ask for. However there are many factor to consider. On 1 October 2008 new rules applied for householders wanting to hard surface over their front gardens. If the surface to be covered is more than five square metres, you are required to get planning permission for laying traditional, impermeable driveways that do not control rainwater running off onto roads. This will apply to new driveways, drive extensions or drive replacements. You will not need planning permission if the surface to be covered is less than five square metres, a new or replacement driveway of any size uses permeable (or porous) surfacing which allows water to drain through, such as gravel, permeable concrete block paving or porous asphalt, or if the rainwater is directed to a lawn or border to drain naturally.
No retrospective planning consent is needed for existing driveways and hard surfaces.
Solutions available to avoid the need for planning permission: permeable paving, sustainable drainage systems, porous asphalt, drivegrid driveway system, gravel or a mainly green, vegetated area, traditional hard surfaces with controlled run-off to your garden or lawn or to a purpose-built rain garden or soakaway.

Brickwork: whether to replace your front garden wall, pillars, stairs, retaining walls we have the expertise to complete professional work using both new or reclaimed bricks. We can recommend the best finish suit to surrounding.
Traditional uncovered finish or rendered, painted walls can be done for request.

Turf installation: from removing the old turf or unvanted vegetation through breaking ground, creating a perfect, even surface to lay the fresh, quality lawn we make sure every step we do professionally.
To keep your newly installed turf in a good condition aftercare is key factor. We advise and even offer maintenance for the grass layed by our team.
For aftercare cost please contact us.

Plant design   Planting: the last and also very important stage of landscaping projects to create a sustainable all year interest garden.
Whether it's shady, sun exposed, windy or dry, after considering the conditions we can choose the best possible plant selection for your garden.
We collect information (soil test, site survey, customer survey) and make a planting plan including predictions for all year display and the state of the garden after 1, 2, 5 and 10 years time.
The planting design can include aftercare instructions or we can do regular or follow up maintenance.
